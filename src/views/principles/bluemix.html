## Logo Usage
### Exclusion zone No other piece of content can be in the logo's exclusion zone. This is to insure that the logo always has acceptable breathing room; as well as guarantees that the icon and work mark as always associated together. The exclusion zone is equal to the height of the “B” in the work mark. It is shown above by the magenta border around the logo. ### Space between icon and wordmark The space between the icon and wordmark is determined by the trajectory of the outer angles of the right edges of top and bottom hexagons. It is demonstrated in the above image by the dotted lines that make an "X". ### Minimum sizing Including in the use of the logo.

## Logo Misusage In order to maintain logo consistency please do not do any of the following to the logo. The logo should never be modified, transformed or reinterpreted. The logo and icon should remain as defined above - there are no exceptions.

Do not use the old wordmark that includes the capital "M" in "Bluemix"

Do not mess with the general appearance of the logo - no drop-shadows, feather, glow or other effects should be applied.

Do not change the wordmark typeface or font weight.

Do not skew or disproportionally scale the logo.

Do not change the size relationship of the icon and wordmark.

Do not invert the positioning of the icon and wordmark.

Do not use any other colors in the logo or icon other than the color combinations defined in these guidelines.

Do not place the logo on busy backgrounds that make it hard to distinguish.

### Best practices: - Use color with purpose. - Use Color, along with spacing and sizing, to create page hierarchy and focus. - If a brand color is not creating the proper page hierarchy, evaluate all the visual elements (size, padding, pacing) before selecting the use of a non-brand color. - Use contrast to influence the legibility of a design. - Understand visual deficiencies such as low vision and color blindness. - Avoid eye strain (such as color combinations that cause vibration). - Understand cultural associations with colors.
