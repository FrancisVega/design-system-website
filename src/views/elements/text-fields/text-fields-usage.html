{{#markdown}} ### Labels Effective form labeling helps users understand what information to put into the text field. As a space-saving method, often placeholder/hint text is used as a label. This is not best practice, since the interaction of the label disappearing can cause issues for people with cognitive issues or who use a screen magnifier. It also increases cognitive load for all users who would have to remember the purpose of the field after they filled it in with text. - Label must be visible when the field gets focus - The label must be announced to the screen reader on focus - Ensure helper text that appears under a field is read when the assistive technology user stops at the field using aria ### Placeholder text Placeholder text is used to complement a field and provide additional assistance to the user. It is helpful to provide examples of the data format required. Placeholder text should dissapear once the user has inputted data into the text field.
