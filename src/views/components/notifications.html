Format

Size

Generic notifications have a fixed width to maintain consistency, but their height can vary depending on content.
Inline notifications width and height may vary depending on the type of content presented.

Title

All notifications should have titles to give users a quick means of understanding the content of the notification. Titles should match the notification color (see below for color meaning). App, service, and compute resource names should be bolded.

Message

The body or message of the notification must be contained within two lines. Be as descriptive as possible, and try to include troubleshooting actions or next steps. If you can communicate the main message of your notification to the user within the title, a secondary message may not be needed.

Date

Every notification should be time-stamped.
Use the following rules to display time:

  • Less than one hour: "30 minutes ago"
  • Less than 24 hours ago: "3 hours ago"
  • Less than a month ago: "12 days ago"
  • Less than a year ago: "8 months ago"
  • Over a year: Follow ISO 8601 Standards: "2016-01-19"


Color

Green 30: Success

Yellow 30: Warning

Red 30: Error

Blue 20: Informational


Icons

Icons aren't used in generic notifications, due to the space constraints. When icons appear in inline or modal notifications, they are placed to the left of the title. See the Style tab for further specs about icons.


Placement

Generic Notifications

These should always appear in the top right corner, and slide in from off canvas. Actionable notifications do not appear on mobile screen widths.

Inline Notifications

These should appear close to the item that they are related to.

Dismissal

All notifications must have one method of dismissal. Typically, it is a small "x" in the upper right hand corner.
