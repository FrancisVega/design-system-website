{{#markdown}} ## General Dropdown lists are used when the selection of items are of secondary importance. If space is a concern, use dropdowns to minimize distraction. ### Text Limit dropdown content to a single line of text that describes the action it will perform when selected. Do not use multiple lines of text, images, or icons within a dropdown. Sort the items in logical order, such as grouping related options together. ### Length Keep dropdown options succinct. Long dropdown menus that require scrolling make it difficult for users to see all their options in one glance. ## Interaction Dropdowns should appear on-click rather than on hover. Hover-activated dropdowns increase cognitive load on users and are not recommended due to usability issues. Dropdown menus should appear above all other elements within the UI. They can be dismissed by clicking outside of the menu item or on the parent element. Generally, selecting an item from the dropdown menu will also dismiss the menu.
